The timeline of the Cathode Ray Tube

Definitions

A Cathode is an electrode by which electrons enter a system like an electrolytic cell or an electron tube.
A Cathode Ray on the other hand is a beam of electrons leaving the Cathode in a discharge tube or released by a heated filament in certain electron tubes.
A vacuum tube is an electron tube with a near vacuum(the air has been withdrawn/extracted).
A Cathode Ray Tube or CRT is a vacuum tube whereby images are produced when an electron beam strikes a phosphorescent screen http://inventors.about.com/od/cstartinventions/a/CathodeRayTube.htm
